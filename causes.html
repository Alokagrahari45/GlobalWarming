Main Causes of Global Warming

  • Burning Fossil Fuels:
    The largest source of greenhouse gases is the burning of fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and gas for electricity, heat, and transportation.
  • Deforestation:
    Trees absorb CO2, so deforestation reduces Earth's capacity to soak up excess carbon dioxide, accelerating warming.
  • Industrial Emissions:
    Industrial processes release large amounts of greenhouse gases, particularly in the energy, steel, cement, and chemical industries.
  • Agriculture:
    Methane emissions from livestock and rice paddies, as well as nitrous oxide from fertilized soils, contribute significantly to global warming.
