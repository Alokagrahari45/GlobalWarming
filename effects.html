Key Effects of Global Warming

  • Rising Sea Levels:
    Global warming causes glaciers and polar ice caps to melt, contributing to rising sea levels and threatening coastal cities and habitats.
  • More Frequent and Severe Weather:
    Increased heat in the atmosphere and oceans is causing more intense storms, heatwaves, and droughts.
  • Impact on Wildlife:
    Changing ecosystems and habitats disrupt wildlife, causing some species to migrate or face extinction due to temperature changes and loss of habitat.
  • Threat to Human Health:
    Global warming increases the spread of diseases, heat-related illnesses, and respiratory problems due to worsening air quality.
